Cultural Day is an opportunity for our young learners to embrace diversity, learn about different traditions, and celebrate unity in our school. Throughout the day, students will engage in various activities, performances, and presentations showcasing the beauty of different cultures represented among us.

Yoruba

Yoruba culture embodies the essence of the Yoruba people from ancient times. The customs, character, and heritage of the Yoruba serve as the foundation for their sense of identity and belonging.

Igbo

The Igbo culture, deeply rooted in southeastern Nigeria, is renowned for its rich traditions, vibrant ceremonies, and strong community ties. From birth to death, every aspect of life in Igbo society is infused with cultural significance.

Fulani

The Fulani, also known as Fula or Fulbe, are a nomadic and pastoralist ethnic group spread across West and Central Africa, with a rich and diverse cultural heritage deeply rooted in their traditional way of life.
